How to Set Slippage??

In this tutorial, we will provide guidance on how to set slippage when making swaps on PepeXchange. Slippage is the difference between the price you expected and the price you get when making a swap. This is an important setting for managing your risk. We will use MetaMask as an example wallet.



Step 1: Access PepeXchange


• Open the PepeXchange website in your browser and make sure you have connected your wallet.



Step 2: Choose an Asset Pair


• On the PepeXchange page, select the asset pair you want to exchange.

• For example, if you want to exchange BNB for PEPEX, choose the BNB/PEPEX pair.



Step 3: Enter the Amount to Trade


• Click on the box displaying the amount of the asset you want to exchange.

• Enter the amount you want to exchange from the first asset (for example, BNB).



Step 4: Find Slippage Settings


• In the PepeXchange interface, there is usually a “Slippage” or “Slippage Rate” option.

• This is the place where you can set the extent of the price difference that is acceptable to you.

• Generally, you can enter a slippage percentage that you consider safe.



Step 5: Set Slippage


• Enter your desired slippage percentage according to your risk tolerance.

• For example, you can set the slippage at 1% if you are willing to accept a price difference of 1% from the expected price.



Step 6: Check Transaction Details


• PepeXchange will display updated transaction details according to your slippage settings.

• Check these details carefully.



Step 7: Confirm Transaction


• After checking the details carefully, click the "Confirm" button or something similar.

• Confirm the transaction with your wallet.



Step 8: Wait for Confirmation


• Wait until the transaction is confirmed on the blockchain network. The time required for confirmation depends on the level of network congestion.




Vital Records:

Slippage settings are important for managing risk in crypto asset trading.

The smaller the slippage setting, the greater the chance that your transaction will fail if the price difference exceeds that limit.

Very large slippage may result in an exchange price that is far from what you expected.

By following the steps above, you can set the slippage according to your risk tolerance when making a swap. This helps you control your trading results in a fluctuating DeFi environment.
